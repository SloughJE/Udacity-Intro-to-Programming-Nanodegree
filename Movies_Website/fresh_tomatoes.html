Some of my Favorite Movies, with descriptions and trailers

The Wind Will Carry Us

Director: Abbas Kiarostami

Year of release: 1999

Prefer the present to these fine promises. Even a drum sounds melodious from afar.

The Unbearable Lightness of Being

Director: Philip Kaufman

Year of release: 1988

During the Prague Spring, Tomas has a sophisticated lover, Sabina, but becomes smitten with a bookish country girl named Tereza, and the two begin a relationship.

The Fellowship of the Ring

Director: Peter Jackson

Year of release: 2001

In a small village in the Shire a young Hobbit named Frodo (Elijah Wood) has been entrusted with an ancient Ring. Now he must embark on an epic quest to the Cracks of Doom in order to destroy it.

Sonnenschein

Director: Istvan Szabo

Year of release: 1999

The film follows a Jewish family living in Hungary through three generations, rising from humble beginnings to positions of wealth and power in the crumbling Austro-Hungarian Empire.

Closely Watched Trains

Director: Jiri Menzel

Year of release: 1966

At a village railway station in occupied Czechoslovakia, a bumbling dispatcher's apprentice longs to liberate himself from his virginity.

Where the Green Ants Dream

Director: Werner Herzog

Year of release: 1984

In the wilds of Australia, aboriginal tribe culture is threatened by a giant corporation that wants to mine in one of the holiest sites.

Fitzcarraldo

Director: Werner Herzog

Year of release: 1982

A would-be rubber baron Brian Sweeney Fitzgerald, an Irishman known in Peru as Fitzcarraldo, is determined to transport a steamship over a steep hill in order to access a rich rubber territory in the Amazon Basin.

Zelary

Director: Ondrej Trojan

Year of release: 2003

A nurse and her surgeon-lover are part of a resistance movement in 1940s Czechoslovakia. When they are discovered, her lover flees and she must find a place to hide. A patient whose life she saved, a man from a remote mountain village where time stopped 150 years ago, agrees to hide her as his wife.

Taste of Cherry

Director: Abbas Kiarostami

Year of release: 1997

Mr. Badii (Homayoun Ershadi) drives around Tehran in hopes of finding someone willing to burry him after he commits suicide. He has little luck finding an accomplice willing to bury him, suicide is forbidden under Islamic law and this is what puts him in this desperate situation.
